With the definite article:
ar + an → ar an + urú (Ulster) vs. ar + séimhiú elsewhere
ar an bhord (Connacht) / ar an bhord nó ar an bord (Ulster — lenition preferred in speech)

ag an, chuig an, le — no mutation before definite article in Ulster Irish in practice.
den, don, sa/san, faoin, ón, tríd an — preposition fused with an.
Réamhfhocail Chomhshuite
Compound prepositions govern the genitive case of the following noun. They do not take prepositional pronouns — instead, use a personal pronoun after the component simple preposition.
Genitive rule: After a compound preposition, use the genitive form of the noun. E.g. os comhair an tí (in front of the house), i ndiaidh na hoíche (after the night).

Personal pronouns: os mo chomhair · os do chomhair · os a chomhair · os a comhair · os ár gcomhair

Spás agus Treo — Space & Direction

RéamhfhocalCiallSampla
ar aghaidh + gen in front of; opposite; facing Tá sé ar aghaidh na scoile. It's opposite the school. Chuaigh sé ar aghaidh leis an obair. He continued with the work.
ar chúl + gen behind, at the back of Tá an gairdín ar chúl an tí. The garden is behind the house.
os comhair + gen in front of; opposite; facing Bhí sé ina sheasamh os mo chomhair. He was standing in front of me.
os cionn + gen above; over; more than Tá teach mór os cionn na habhann. There's a big house above the river. Bhí os cionn céad duine ann. There were more than a hundred people there.
faoi bhun + gen below; under; less than Tá sé faoi bhun míle uainn. It's less than a mile from us.
in aice le + gen near; beside; next to Bhí sí ina suí in aice liom. She was sitting beside me.
le taobh + gen beside; alongside; compared with Tá teach beag le taobh na habhann. There's a small house alongside the river.
thart ar around; approximately; about Bhí thart ar fiche duine ann. There were about twenty people there.

Am — Time

RéamhfhocalCiallSampla
ar feadh + gen for (a period of time); throughout Ar feadh i bhfad, ní raibh fhios agam. For a long time, I didn't know. Bhí sé ar feadh uaire ann. He was there for an hour.
i gcaitheamh + gen during; throughout; in the course of I gcaitheamh na bliana, tháinig athrú mór. During the year, a big change came.
le linn + gen during; at the time of Le linn na cogaidh, bhí ganntanas bia ann. During the war, there was a food shortage.
i ndiaidh + gen after; following; behind Chuaigh sí i ndiaidh a máthar. She took after her mother. I ndiaidh na hoíche, tháinig an mhaidin. After the night, morning came.

Cúis, Cuspóir & Caoi — Reason, Purpose & Manner

RéamhfhocalCiallSampla
de bharr + gen because of; due to; on account of Bhí sé deacair de bharr na drochaimsire. It was difficult because of the bad weather.
mar gheall ar + gen because of; on account of; about Tá imní orm mar gheall air. I'm worried about it.
ar son + gen for the sake of; for (benefit) Ar son Dé, bí ciúin! For God's sake, be quiet! Rinne sé é ar son an phobail. He did it for the sake of the community.
ar mhaithe le + gen for the good of; for the benefit of Rinne sé é ar mhaithe leis an phobal. He did it for the good of the community.
de réir + gen according to; in accordance with De réir mo thuisceana, tá sé ceart. According to my understanding, it's correct.
ar nós + gen like; in the manner of; as Tá sé ar nós a athar. He's like his father. D'imigh sé ar nós na gaoithe. He left like the wind.
in ionad + gen instead of; in place of In ionad dul ann, d'fhan sé sa bhaile. Instead of going there, he stayed at home.
in éadan + gen against; opposing Bhí siad in éadan na hathraithe. They were against the changes.

Áit i Measc — Location Among

RéamhfhocalCiallSampla
i lár + gen in the middle of; in the centre of I lár na cathrach atá sé. It's in the middle of the city.
i measc + gen among; amid; in the midst of Bhí siad i measc na ndaoine. They were among the people.
Personal pronoun forms with compound prepositions:
os mo chomhair · os do chomhair · os a chomhair (m.) · os a comhair (f.) · os ár gcomhair · os bhur gcomhair · os a gcomhair

ar mo shon · ar do shon · ar a shon · ar a son · ar ár son · ar bhur son · ar a son

The pattern: the personal possessive adjective (mo, do, a…) is inserted between the parts of the compound.
Comhrá Laethúil
Everyday conversational lines — read and internalise these. Ulster Irish forms highlighted where they differ from standard.

Ag — Possession & State

01
Níl a fhios agam.
I don't know.
Core phrase. Níl fhios agam (without the a) also heard in Ulster.
ag
02
An bhfuil airgead agat?
Do you have money?
Possession with ag — Irish doesn't use a verb "to have".
ag
03
Cad é atá agat inniu?
What have you on today? / What are you up to today?
Cad é — Ulster form of cad / céard.
ag · Ulster

Ar — Emotions & States

04
Tá tuirse mhór orm.
I'm very tired.
Emotions and physical states sit on the person in Irish. tuirse + orm — tiredness on me.
ar
05
Cad é atá ort? / Cad atá cearr ort?
What's wrong with you? / What's the matter?
ar
06
Bhí áthas mór orm é sin a chloisteáil.
I was very glad to hear that.
áthas orm — gladness on me. Adjust: brón orm (sadness), imní orm (worry), náire orm (shame).
ar
07
Chuir mé glao air.
I called him / I rang him.
glao a chur ar dhuine — to make a call on someone.
ar

Le — With & Belonging

08
Bhí muid le chéile aréir.
We were together last night.
le chéile — together (with each other). Common everyday phrase.
le
09
Is liom féin é.
It's mine. / It belongs to me.
Ownership: le + pronoun. Is leatsa é — it's yours.
le
10
Labhair mé leis ar maidin.
I spoke to him this morning.
labhairt le duine — to speak to someone (ar in older/formal registers).
le

Do — Giving & Indirect Object

11
Thug sé leabhar domh.
He gave me a book.
Ulster form: domh — not dom. The /v/ sound is audible: dúv.
do · Ulster
12
Gabh mo leithscéal. Cad é a dúirt tú?
Excuse me. What did you say?
Gabh mo leithscéal — Ulster everyday formula for "excuse me / pardon".
Ulster

Ó — From & Since

13
Ní fhaca mé thú ó shin.
I haven't seen you since then.
ó
14
Chuala mé uaidh ar maidin.
I heard from him this morning.
ó

I — In & Into

15
Tá sé ina chónaí i mBéal Feirste.
He lives in Belfast.
Eclipsis: i + B → i mB. Also: i gContae Aontroma, i nDoire, i nGaillimh.
i + urú
16
Tá sé ag obair ann le fada.
He's been working there for a long time.
ann = the 3rd person masculine prepositional pronoun of i, meaning "in it / there".
i

Compound Prepositions in Conversation

17
Bhí sé ina sheasamh os mo chomhair.
He was standing in front of me / facing me.
os comhair
18
Bhí sé deacair de bharr na drochaimsire.
It was difficult because of the bad weather.
de bharr
19
Tá imní orm mar gheall air.
I'm worried about it.
imní + orm — worry on me. mar gheall ar — because of / about.
mar gheall ar
20
Tá sé ar nós a athar.
He's like his father.
ar nós
21
In ionad dul ann, d'fhan sé sa bhaile.
Instead of going there, he stayed at home.
in ionad + verbal noun = "instead of doing". Very common structure.
in ionad
22
Chuaigh sí i ndiaidh a máthar.
She took after her mother.
Also: dul i ndiaidh duine — to follow someone / to go after someone.
i ndiaidh
23
Bhí thart ar fiche duine ann.
There were about twenty people there.
thart ar
24
De réir mo thuisceana, tá sé ceart.
According to my understanding, it's correct.
de réir
25
D'imigh sé ar nós na gaoithe.
He left like the wind. (He was off in a flash.)
Set phrase — very expressive. gaoth → gen. sg. gaoithe.
ar nós · idiom
26
Ar son Dé, éist liom!
For God's sake, listen to me!
éist le duine — to listen to someone. ar son + genitive.
ar son
27
Bhí siad in éadan na hathraithe.
They were against the changes.
in éadan
28
Bhí sé ann ar feadh i bhfad.
He was there for a long time.
ar feadh i bhfad — for a long time (set phrase; "i bhfad" in the genitive position).
ar feadh
29
Le linn na hoíche, tháinig athrú aimsire.
During the night, there was a weather change.
le linn
30
Tchífidh mé thú um Nollaig.
I'll see you around Christmas.
tchífidh — Ulster Irish future of feic (to see). Standard: feicfidh.
